Abstract

Degradation-Agnostic Restoration via Deterministic Latent Transport

Image restoration aims to recover high-quality images from degraded observations, yet existing methods struggle with diverse degradation types. In this work, we propose SLAIR, an all-in-one image restoration framework integrating latent space separation and deterministic transport. We introduce a latent-space separation approach that ensures stable, distinct representations of degraded and clean images, facilitating effective flow matching. By learning continuous transport flow in a latent space via an ordinary differential equation (ODE), our method enables deterministic restoration without relying on degradation labels or stochastic sampling. While the restoration is performed in the latent space, a high-frequency refinement module is introduced into the latent decoder to handle detail distortion during decoding. Experiments demonstrate that SLAIR outperforms existing approaches in terms of both perceptual quality and computational efficiency, making it a robust solution for scalable, degradation-agnostic image restoration.

Method

Three-Stage Framework

Fig. 2 - SLAIR first structures degraded and clean latents, then learns deterministic ODE transport with flow matching, and finally restores high-frequency details through a wavelet-aware VAE decoder.

1

Degradation-Aware Latent Separation

The paper shows that original VAE latents entangle degraded and clean samples, making the transport direction ambiguous. SLAIR fine-tunes the VAE with binary and degradation-subtype classification losses, enlarging the margin between zdeg and zgt while preserving reconstruction quality.

2

Deterministic Conditional Transport

Instead of estimating degradation labels or sampling from random noise, SLAIR learns a conditional velocity field in latent space. A LightningDiT backbone solves the ODE from degraded latents to clean latents, yielding a stable degradation-agnostic restoration path.

3

High-Frequency Details Refinement

Latent restoration can lose fine structures during decoding. SLAIR injects pyramid VAE encoder features through fusion blocks and constrains the decoder with a DWT high-frequency loss, recovering sharp edges while suppressing reintroduced rain, moire, haze, and other artifacts.

Quantitative Benchmarks

State-of-the-Art on Setting (i) & Setting (ii)

SLAIR is evaluated under two paper settings: Setting (i) is the comprehensive benchmark over Rain1400, Outdoor-Rain, Raindrop, UHDSnow, SOTS, BSD68, LoL, UHDM, and HDR-Eye; Setting (ii) is the standard 5-task all-in-one benchmark over BSD68, GoPro, LoL, Rain100L, and SOTS.
