Abstract

Degradation-Agnostic Restoration via Deterministic Latent Transport

Image restoration aims to recover high-quality images from degraded observations, yet existing methods struggle with diverse degradation types. In this work, we propose SLAIR, an all-in-one image restoration framework integrating latent space separation and deterministic transport. We introduce a latent-space separation approach that ensures stable, distinct representations of degraded and clean images, facilitating effective flow matching. By learning continuous transport flow in a latent space via an ordinary differential equation (ODE), our method enables deterministic restoration without relying on degradation labels or stochastic sampling. While the restoration is performed in the latent space, a high-frequency refinement module is introduced into the latent decoder to handle detail distortion during decoding. Experiments demonstrate that SLAIR outperforms existing approaches in terms of both perceptual quality and computational efficiency, making it a robust solution for scalable, degradation-agnostic image restoration.

Method

Three-Stage Framework

Fig. 2 — SLAIR pipeline. A degraded image is encoded via VAE, structured via latent separation loss, transported deterministically through flow matching (LightningDiT backbone), then decoded with wavelet-based high-frequency refinement.

🗂️

① Latent Space Separation

We fine-tune the VAE with a contrastive classification loss to maximize inter-manifold distance between degraded and clean latents. This structured geometry simplifies transport dynamics and stabilizes training.

🌊

② Deterministic Flow Matching

A LightningDiT-based velocity field learns continuous ODE transport from zdeg → zgt. No degradation labels needed, no stochastic sampling—just a single deterministic ODE solve at inference.

✨

③ High-Frequency Refinement

A DWT-based fusion block in the VAE decoder injects restored high-frequency details before final decoding, eliminating latent-to-pixel distortion and enabling arbitrary-resolution inference.
